<p><p>The smoked haddock market is divided into two primary types: hot-smoked and cold-smoked. Hot-smoked haddock is prepared by cooking the fish during the smoking process, resulting in a flaky texture and a robust flavor, making it popular for ready-to-eat meals. In contrast, cold-smoked haddock undergoes a lower-temperature smoking process, preserving its raw texture while imparting a delicate smoky flavor, suitable for dishes where a subtler taste is desired. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and culinary applications.</p></p>
