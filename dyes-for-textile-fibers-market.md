<p><p>The textile dyes market includes various types tailored for specific fiber properties and applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, providing vibrant colors. Reactive dyes form a direct bond with fibers, ideal for cotton and cellulose materials. Sulfur dyes offer deep, dark shades, especially for denim, while vat dyes provide excellent lightfastness, commonly used for high-quality cotton. Acid dyes are designed for protein fibers like wool and silk, and other dyes encompass less common options for specialized applications.</p></p>
