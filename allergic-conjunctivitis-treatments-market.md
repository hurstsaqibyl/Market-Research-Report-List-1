<p><p>The allergic conjunctivitis treatments market can be segmented into two main types: acute and chronic allergic conjunctivitis. Acute allergic conjunctivitis is characterized by sudden onset and is often triggered by specific allergens, requiring immediate relief through antihistamines, corticosteroids, or decongestants. In contrast, chronic allergic conjunctivitis involves persistent symptoms due to long-term exposure to allergens and typically necessitates ongoing management with treatment options such as mast cell stabilizers and immunotherapy. Each type demands tailored therapeutic approaches for effective symptom control.</p></p>
