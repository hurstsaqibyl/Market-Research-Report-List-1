<p><p>The petroleum sorbents market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic sorbents and cellulose sorbents. Inorganic sorbents, made from materials like clay, silica, and other minerals, offer high absorbency and thermal stability, making them suitable for various spill remediation applications. Cellulose sorbents, derived from natural fibers, are biodegradable and effective for absorbing oil spills while being environmentally friendly. Both types cater to diverse sectors, including industrial, marine, and environmental protection, addressing the need for efficient oil spill management solutions.</p></p>
